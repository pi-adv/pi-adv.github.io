Section 0 - Preface

First off, addressing the misconceptions of Cyberpatriot and the competition themselves. Many competitiors describe Cyberpatriot as a Windows and Linux system-hardening competition. Which is semi-correct. How Hypernova describes Cyberpatriot is a high school incident response and remediation competition.

Secondly, the other misconception of the competition is the need to catch all of the vulnerabilities. Which this problem is described as simply created scripts and checklists and throwing it at the image, hoping for some points to be knocked out of it, saying that these vulnerabilities are stored in a CyberPatriot Vulnerability Bank that we make in our own minds and checklists. Hypernova states that it's not completely wrong to do this but it is not the most effective way to approach a competition.

Section 1 - Approaching the Competition

Hypernova stated that they are not releasing their notes with the checklists and practices that they used. This makes complete sense but they do give some tips to develop our own checklist and practices.

Method 1 - Research

Research is crucial to a Cybertitan team. Hypernova even specifies that research is important before, during and after the competition. The way you research is also specified, this being that you SHOULD NOT just google settings for a service than pasting those random settings. This practice should be used but we have never really used it. What's recommended to do is to search up these settings but also apply the settings, by setting up an environnment and then fooling around with the setting and configurations that you should have found.

Researching effectively is extremely important. Hypernova says during competition you will encounter something that will be messed with or thrown at you that won't work. They suggest you develop skills to be able to search effectively for any critical service.

Keeping note of what you struggled with and then finding out how to solve the issue you couldn't figure out in competition. Hypernova helpfully provided a list of critical services that we should look into and know how to configure.

These services include:

  1. HTTP/S Services

Section 2 - Respecting the Challenge

Hypernova highlights that many people tend to take shortcuts when preparing and competiting. There is a sort of right way to do things, such as:

  1. Creating proper configurations for servers and ensuring the changes apply to the certain service you are configuring.
  2. Testing before, don't go into the competition testing out things for the first time.
  3. Dev testing your programs for errors.

Section 3 - Baselining and Meld

Baselining represents comparing clean or secured configurations and environments to the ones in the challenge image. Hypernova gives an example when they went on a linux image, they would spend the first hour comparing permission values and changes that have appeared in the system. Which is pretty genius since it can highlight a whole bunch of issues that potentially could be solved.

The tool used to compare files and configurations is something called Meld which Hypernova, says that it is a visually based diffing and merging tool for files and directories that works in Linux and Windows.

It can be used in Windows but Hypernova outlines that it is more difficult to set a base line. This could be because it's more difficult to get a list of every single directory, or purely because Linux and Windows work differently.

Section 4 - Automation (Consistency vs. Speed)

Script apparently are extremely imporant. Hypernova has made it clear that scripts could be the piece that we are missing. Though they give a tip for new competitors, and it's to build for consistency rather than speed. The problems with the speed method is:

  • Spending too much too optimizing. Lose the purpose of the script. Build a quality script.
  • Only building the script, not having any other plan.

Hypernova recommeneds these methods on scripting:

  • Focus on the purpose of the script.
  • Keep up with developement.
  • Adapt your plan with the results and incorperate your script

Section 5 - Testing and Gameplans

It's important to keep testing gameplans, and scripts. Hypernova, states that it is not rare to see a teams script breaking. Putting through a lot of testing can decide between your script working and breaking on competition day. Hypernova puts it simply, play smart.

Section 6 - Team Composition

One of the best advice that Hypernova provides is the team setup advice that he gives. Hypernova recalls certain moments when teams crumbled under the pressure due to how their team is setup.

Hypernova then explains a six person team, which currently isn't possible. Not just because for our Cybertitan team cannot fit six members but according to the rules we can only have five members on a team competing at the same time. They explain how it is beneficial to have a sixth member due to how many challenges lie in the competition but unfortunately we cannot compete with six members.

Hypernova still has a great team composition template that we should take into account

  • Windows Competitors (2 Members)
    • First three rounds have two windows images, rather than one linux.
    • Windows can be more tricky, difficult to get automation.
    • Windows challenges tend to be more problematic.
  • Linux Competitor (1 Member)
    • Linux only has one image in the first three rounds.
    • Linux is more managable in than Windows. Much more automation potential.
  • Linux/Extra Competitor (1 Member)
    • The role of this competitor is to provide assistance to the primary linux competitor.
    • Also, pursuing any extra points are or become available.
  • Dedicated Cisco Competitor (1 Member)
    • This competitor is going to be focused on Cisco Packet Tracer and gathering all the points that come through that route.
  • 'Jack of All Trades' Competitor (NOT ALLLOWED DUE TO LIMIT OF 5 MEMEBERS 1 Member (Subsitute))
    • Helps with everyother competitor to make the process smooth and simple.
